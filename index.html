Who I Am

A passionate security researcher with a deep fascination for understanding how things work under the hood.

My journey into low-level programming began with a simple curiosity: what happens when software interacts with hardware? That question led me down a rabbit hole of assembly language, memory management, and reverse engineering.

Today, I focus on analyzing complex systems, identifying vulnerabilities, and developing tools that push the boundaries of what's possible in system-level programming. I believe in writing code that is not just functional, but elegant and efficient.

When I'm not dissecting binaries or exploring system internals, you'll find me contributing to security research or optimizing performance-critical applications.
